Three-part series on the question "what are mental illnesses, really".
Casual debates around this abound — "what even is normal", etc.,
but then people still go out and try getting a black-and-white diagnosis of ADHD.
This series of posts does a great job exploring the topic.
The [first part](https://www.astralcodexten.com/p/ontology-of-psychiatric-conditions) covers taxometrics, a set of methods you use to figure out whether some condition is more likely to be caused by something underlying you have or don't have, or is just the tail end of trait variation.
The [second part](https://www.astralcodexten.com/p/ontology-of-psychiatric-conditions-34e) looks at psychiatric conditions through the lens of dynamical systems.
The [third part](https://www.astralcodexten.com/p/ontology-of-psychiatric-conditions-653) asks whether conditions are more likely to be a failure of evolution, or one side of some tradeoff.
