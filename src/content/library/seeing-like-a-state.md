Maybe more meme than book at this point. For the gist, read [vgr's post](https://www.ribbonfarm.com/2010/07/26/a-big-little-idea-called-legibility/) about the central idea of "legibility".
He hints at parallels in tech (waterfall project planning), but I often find myself thinking about this when
someone wants to add 20 strictly-validated form fields across 15 rigid workflow steps in some tool,
hoping it will make reality conform to the platonic ideal of the process,
but most likely just making people hate their job and put everything important in the unstructured "notes" field instead.
